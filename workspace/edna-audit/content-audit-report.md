1. **AI/App Development pivot is timely** — The recent events and courses on vibe coding, Lovable, Supabase, and AI agents are aligned with where the market is going. This is genuinely forward-thinking content.

2. **Pulses (short video clips)** — 45+ bite-sized videos on the dashboard covering current AI topics. Good for engagement and positioning as thought leadership.

## 3. CRITICAL ISSUES & GAPS 🔴

### A. Dangerously Small Course Library (12 courses)
This is the #1 problem. **12 courses is not a viable subscription library.**
- DataCamp has 400+ courses
- Coursera has thousands
- Even niche players like Maven Analytics have 40+
- A subscriber paying monthly expects breadth AND depth

**Impact:** This is likely the primary driver of churn. Once someone finishes 2-3 relevant courses, there's no reason to stay subscribed.

### B. Power BI Overconcentration
- 5 of 12 courses are Power BI focused (42%)
- 8 of 14 learning paths involve Power BI
- The platform's roots as a "Power BI training" brand are clear, but the market has moved on
- Power BI is mature — people are learning it from Microsoft Learn (free) or Udemy ($15 courses)

**Risk:** Power BI alone is not a compelling subscription proposition in 2026.

### C. Missing In-Demand Content Categories
These are table-stakes topics for any data/AI learning platform in 2026 that are **completely absent**:

| Missing Topic | Market Demand | Competitor Coverage |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Generative AI / LLMs** (using, fine-tuning, prompt engineering courses) | Extremely high | DataCamp, Coursera, Udemy all have extensive coverage |
| **Machine Learning fundamentals** | High | Every major platform |
| **Data Engineering** (dbt, Airflow, pipelines) | Very high | DataCamp, Coursera |
| **Tableau / Looker** | High | Maven, DataCamp, Coursera |
| **Excel / Google Sheets deep dives** | Perennial demand | Everywhere |
| **Cloud platforms** (AWS/Azure/GCP data services) | High | Coursera, A Cloud Guru |
| **AI Agent development** (beyond vibe coding) | Emerging but growing fast | Sparse — opportunity |
| **Statistics / Math foundations** | Evergreen | Everywhere |
| **Business Intelligence strategy** | Medium-high | Scattered |
| **Certification prep** (PL-300, DP-600, AWS certs) | High — drives subscriptions | DataCamp, Coursera |

### D. Content Update Frequency Is Misleading
Many courses show recent "Last updated" dates (Feb 2026), but the **core content appears to be the same Power BI material from years ago** with cosmetic updates. The actual substance needs evaluation — a "last updated" date doesn't mean the content is fresh.

### E. Assessments Feel Auto-Generated & Disconnected
- The 15 assessments cover web development and Selenium — topics with **zero corresponding courses**
- No Power BI, DAX, Python, or SQL assessments (the actual course topics)
- Authored by "EDNA AI" and "EDNA Experts" — feels like they were bulk-generated
- No XP awarded for assessments (all show "—")

### F. No Free/Freemium Content Strategy
- No visible free courses or free tier to attract new users
- Competitors offer substantial free content as a funnel (DataCamp has free courses, Coursera has audit mode)
- There IS a "free" filter in the sort options but unclear what content qualifies

### G. Events Are All Past & Sam-Led
- All 12 visible events are from early-mid 2025
- All led by Sam McKay — no guest experts or industry practitioners
- No upcoming events scheduled
- Events are a key engagement/retention tool and they've gone silent

### H. Podcast Dates Stopped at Dec 2025
- 16 podcasts, all from Aug-Dec 2025
- No 2026 episodes visible — 2 months of silence
- Regular fresh content is essential for retention

## 5. RECOMMENDATIONS: WHAT WOULD BRING SUBSCRIBERS BACK

### 🔴 URGENT (Do Now)

**1. Reframe the Value Proposition Away From "Courses"**
- You have 12 courses. You're not a course platform. Stop trying to be one.
- You ARE an **AI-powered data skills ecosystem** — Mentor, KnowCode, Power Vibes, Builders, and learning content.
- The subscription value should be: "Access to AI tools + community + curated learning" — not "course library"

**2. Resume Regular Content Production**
- Restart podcasts (weekly)
- Schedule live events (bi-weekly minimum)
- Add new courses monthly (even short 30-60 min ones)
- Silence = churn

**3. Fix the Assessment Disconnect**
- Create assessments that match actual courses (Power BI, DAX, Python, SQL)
- Remove or hide the random web dev/Selenium assessments
- Award XP for assessments

### 🟡 HIGH PRIORITY (Next 1-3 Months)

**4. Add 5-10 New Courses in High-Demand Areas:**
- Generative AI for Data Professionals (using ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini for data work)
- Building AI Agents (hands-on, not just conceptual)
- Microsoft Fabric Deep Dive
- Python for AI App Development
- Prompt Engineering Masterclass
- Data Storytelling & Presentation
- SQL for Analytics (intermediate-advanced)
- Introduction to Machine Learning

**5. Create a Certification Program**
- "EDNA Certified AI Builder" or "EDNA Data Analyst"
- Combine courses + assessments + challenge completion
- This gives subscribers a tangible goal and reason to stay
- Also serves as a marketing asset

**6. Launch a Free Tier**
- 2-3 free courses + limited Mentor access
- This is your acquisition funnel
- Convert free users to paid with tool access + advanced content

### 🟢 STRATEGIC (3-6 Months)

**7. Bring In External Instructors**
- Sam can't carry 12 courses + events + podcasts + blog alone
- Partner with practitioners, Microsoft MVPs, AI builders
- Guest instructors bring their audience with them

**8. Add Project-Based Learning Tracks**
- "Build a Data Agent from Scratch" (multi-week project)
- "Power BI to Production Dashboard" (end-to-end)
- These keep subscribers engaged longer than course-watching

**9. Corporate/Team Play**
- The Manager Dashboard exists — lean into B2B
- Team onboarding paths, progress reporting, custom content
- B2B = predictable revenue + lower churn

**10. Win-Back Campaign**
- For lapsed subscribers: "Here's what's new since you left"
- Highlight the AI tools (Mentor, KnowCode) — many may not know they exist
- Offer a "come back for 1 month free" to re-engage

## 6. THE MISSING LINK — TL;DR

**The gap isn't one thing — it's a positioning mismatch.**

EDNA Learn is selling itself as a course platform but only has 12 courses. That's a losing proposition against DataCamp (400 courses at lower price) and Coursera (university credentials).

**The winning play:** Reposition as the platform where data professionals **build with AI** — not just learn about it. The Mentor tools, KnowCode, community, and forward-thinking AI/agent content are genuinely unique. No competitor has that combination.

The courses should support and complement the tools, not be the main attraction. Make the AI ecosystem the star. The learning content is the supporting cast.

**To keep current subscribers:** Regular fresh content (events, podcasts, new courses) + certification program + deeper project-based tracks.

**To win back lapsed:** Showcase the evolved AI toolset + offer limited-time free access.

**To attract new:** Free tier with course samples + Mentor access + aggressive content marketing around AI builder skills.
